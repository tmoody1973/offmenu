## The Hook Formula

**Template 1: The Origin Story**
```
"[The chef/owner] [did something unexpected] before opening this [cuisine] spot."

Example: "The chef staged at Noma before returning to his grandmother's recipes."
```

**Template 2: The Philosophy**
```
"[Restaurant] doesn't serve [cuisine]â€”they serve [unique perspective]."

Example: "Kasama doesn't serve Filipino foodâ€”they serve memory."
```

**Template 3: The Transformation**
```
"What was once a [former use] is now a [current vibe] destination."

Example: "What was once a Polish dive bar is now a whiskey temple."
```

## The Story Structure

**Paragraph 1: The Setup (1-2 sentences)**
- Context about the restaurant/owner
- The "why" behind the place

**Paragraph 2: The Details (2-3 sentences)**
- Specifics about what they do
- Techniques, ingredients, approach
- What makes it different

**Paragraph 3: The Experience (1-2 sentences)**
- What diners can expect
- The emotional payoff

## Must Try Formats

**Format 1: The Signature**
```
"The [dish] that [reason it's famous]. [Preparation detail]."

Example: "The carnitas taco that built the truck empire. 
Slow-cooked in lard for 12 hours."
```

**Format 2: The Hidden Gem**
```
"[Dish] - [why it's special]. [Unexpected detail]."

Example: "The ube crinkle cookie from the bakery counter. 
Most people come for dinner, they should come for this."
```

**Format 3: The Adventure**
```
"For the brave: [unusual dish]. [What makes it worth trying]."

Example: "For the brave: Sisig made with pork face. 
Sizzling, citrusy, and utterly addictive."
```
